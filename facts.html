What is Alzheimer's disease?

  • Most common form of dementia - the cognitive decline in brain functions, such as memory and judgment, to the point where it interferes with daily life
  • Progressive disease that destroys brain functions, beginning with the loss of memory and thinking skills to eventually the loss of communication and movement
  • Injured neurons and brain cells in the brain, cutting off connections and eventually causing areas of the brain to shrink
  • Loss of brain functions lead to other problems within the body such as malnutrition or infections, which eventually lead to death
  • There is currently no cure, but there is medication to temporarily slow symptoms

What causes Alzheimer’s?

  • Scientists do not have a full answer
  • A variety of factors that can lead to increased risk are:
    1. Age
    2. Genetics
    3. Lifestyle

Research has shown that preserving a healthy lifestyle (nutritious diet and exercise) can reduce the risk of developing Alzheimer’s as well as other diseases such as cancer, heart disease, and diabetes.

Common Misconceptions:

  • Alzheimer’s is just memory loss and is not fatal
    • No one has survived this disease. Alzheimer’s disease destroys brain cells and slowly prevents a person from performing daily life functions.
  • There are enough funds for Alzheimer’s research
    • As shown in the graph, Alzheimer’s research does not receive enough funding compared to research for other chronic diseases.
